Cron Jobs in AWS 1: EB Workers

Posted on Feb 01, 2018 by Engin Dogusoy 2 mins read

Cron jobs are inevitable parts of any infrastructure; they are things that you want to run on certain periods, things you want to offload to calm hours, things that glue your services to each other, keep costs down and your sanity intact.

Even though the term “cron job” may seem to describe a pretty specific set of characteristics; mainly being repetitive and scheduled, it is still a pretty generic term. Before designing and deploying a cron job, you should think about characteristics that differentiate jobs from each other.

  • How frequently should it run?
  • How long does it take?
  • What are the maximum requirements? (CPU, memory, etc.)
  • Does it consist of multiple smaller jobs or is it a single big task?
    • Are there any dependencies between these small jobs?
    • Does prioritization play a role?
    • Can we run them in parallel?

Your solution will depend heavily on the answers and even though you may not have exact answers for them, you should invest in a preliminary profiling effort to have some ballpark estimations.

In this series of articles I want to explore various ways of handling cron jobs in Amazon Web Services (AWS). I will start with Elastic Beanstalk Workers, one of the many ways, improve on it until I feel like it is a good solution for a wide enough subset of cron jobs and then move on a new subset.
